The Amazing Spider-Man

     The second Spider-Man to hit the screen, Andrew Garfield's was the second Spider-Man to be featured on the big screen. This version of Spider-Man was given mnemonic used by Spider-Man in the comics: The Amazing Spiderman. This Spiderman's first movie came out in 2012, and was met with good reviews from audiences and critics, alike, marking the beginning of another new franchise. 

 

The Spider-Man

This version of Spider-Man was a more grounded and realistic version of the character.

  • A key characteristic of this franchise was that it leaned into some of the darker themes that were associated with Spider-Man.
    • The movie showed how Peter Parker lost his parents, and had to go live with Aunt and Uncle, before losing his Uncle, as well :( .
  • The science-fiction plot for this movie was also more grounded, things didn't magically happen; The movie tried to explain the science behind lots of the plot, to a certain extent. 
  • While it did lean into the darker themes, the movie also leaned into the funny and nerdy side of Peter Parker.
    Andrew Garfield a refreshing take on what one would except for a nerdy kid in high-school, in my opinion. 

 

 

The Sequel

Following the success of "The amazing Spider-Man", a sequel was ordered, and the amazing Spider-Man 2 was created. 

  • This movie focused on the emotional fallout of the last movie on Peter Parker, and how he struggled to keep a line between his Parker and Spider life. 
  • Making his life even more difficult is the arrival of a classic Spider-Man villain: Electro.
  • Aside from the personal life of Spider-Man, a large part of the movie is dealt with the birth of Electro and Spider-Man fighting him, before ultimately defeating him. 
  • However, that's not all folks, because is a surprising turn of events, the movie features another villain at the end: The Green Goblin. 
    • Remeber how I said this iteration tried to be realistic and grounded with it's plot, well they really did that for the Green Goblin, as the character almost looks like a goblin in this movie. 
  • Appearing from almost nowhere, this villain comes in to destory Peter's heart: His love interest Gwen Stacy.  

 

The ending for the movie-franchise, and the sequel  will not be spoiled here, but after the movie released, it wasn't met with the best reviews. Adding onto the bad reviews, due to some internal studio conflicts, a third movie in this franchise wan't created, and the entire franchise was cancelled. 

 

This isn't the end though, the adventure for Spider-Man continues in Spider-Man: No Way Home.
